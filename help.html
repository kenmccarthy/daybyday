Getting Started

Day by Day is designed to help you track medications, symptoms, and wellbeing during chemotherapy treatment cycles. The app works entirely on your device ‚Äì no account needed, and your data stays private.

Adding to Your Home Screen

For the best experience, add Day by Day to your phone's home screen:

  • iPhone/iPad: Tap the Share button (square with arrow) in Safari, then "Add to Home Screen"
  • Android: Tap the menu (three dots) in Chrome, then "Add to Home Screen"

Once added, the app opens full-screen like a native app and works offline.

Initial Setup

Before you start, go to Settings and:

  • Enter your name (your first name will be used in greetings)
  • Add your email address for daily summaries
  • Optionally add your date of birth and patient/hospital number
  • Set your cycle dates (Day 0 = chemotherapy day)
  • Add emergency contact numbers
  • Review and adjust medications if needed

Today View

The Today view is your daily dashboard, showing everything relevant to the current day.

What You'll See

  • Daily Quote: An inspirational message that changes each day
  • Emergency Contacts: Quick-dial numbers (if configured)
  • Scheduled Medications: Organised by time of day
  • As-Needed Medications: PRN medications with counters
  • Symptom Check-in: Temperature, weight, and how you're feeling
  • Notes: Space for observations
  • Food & Drink: Space for food and drink notes
  • Today's Win: A place to note something positive

Time Periods

Medications are grouped by time of day, each with a colour:

  • Morning ‚Äì 06:00 to 12:00
  • Afternoon ‚Äì 12:00 to 18:00
  • Evening ‚Äì 18:00 to 22:00
  • Bedtime ‚Äì 22:00 to 00:00

Tracking Medications

Marking as Taken

Tap any medication card to mark it as taken. The app records the exact time and shows a green checkmark. Tap again to undo if you made a mistake.

Medication Info Links

Many medications have an "info" link that opens a patient information page. This is useful for checking side effects or interactions.

As-Needed (PRN) Medications

Use the + and ‚àí buttons to log doses of PRN medications like pain relief or anti-nausea tablets. The counter shows how many you've taken versus the daily maximum.

Progress Bar

The progress bar at the top shows what percentage of your scheduled medications you've taken today. This only counts scheduled medications, not PRN ones.

Daily weight tracking helps identify significant changes. The app shows your change from baseline (your first recorded weight) in both kg and percentage.

Symptom Scales

Rate each symptom from 1 to 5. The colour of each button reflects whether higher or lower is better for that symptom. The symptoms tracked are:

  • Fatigue/Energy ‚Äì 1 = good energy, 5 = exhausted
  • Nausea ‚Äì 1 = none, 5 = severe
  • Pain ‚Äì 1 = none, 5 = severe
  • Appetite ‚Äì 1 = no appetite, 5 = normal
  • Mood ‚Äì 1 = low, 5 = good
  • Mouth Sores ‚Äì 1 = none, 5 = severe
  • Sleep ‚Äì 1 = very poor, 5 = slept well
  • Bowel Function ‚Äì 1 = constipated, 3 = normal, 5 = diarrhoea
  • Concentration ‚Äì 1 = poor, 5 = good

Tap a number to select it. Tap again to deselect. These ratings help you and your care team spot patterns over time.

Today's Win

Even on difficult days, noting something positive can help. It doesn't have to be big ‚Äì maybe you managed a short walk, enjoyed a meal, or had a good conversation.

Calendar View

Cycle Progress

At the top, you'll see how far through the current cycle you are, with a progress bar and day count.

Switching Cycles

Use the cycle pills (Cycle 1, Cycle 2, etc.) to view different cycles. Only cycles with dates set in Settings will be available.

Calendar Days

Each day shows the calendar date and cycle day (D-1, D0, D+1, etc.). Days are colour-coded:

  • Orange background: Chemotherapy day (Day 0)
  • Purple background: Injection day (Day +1)
  • Green border: Today
  • Green background with ‚úì: Completed days

Tap any day to view or edit that day's data in the Today view.

Cycle Trends

Symptom Charts

Below the calendar, you'll find line charts showing how each symptom has varied across the cycle. These charts help you and your care team visualise patterns ‚Äì for example, seeing when fatigue typically peaks or how long nausea lasts after treatment.

Reading the Charts

  • The horizontal axis shows cycle days from Day -1 to Day +20
  • The vertical axis shows symptom levels from 0 to 5
  • Gaps in the line indicate days where no data was recorded
  • Tap or hover on data points to see the exact day and level
Tip
Reviewing these charts before clinic appointments can help you give your oncologist a clear picture of how the cycle went, rather than relying on memory alone.

Settings

Your Details

Enter your name, email address, date of birth, and patient/hospital number. Your first name is used in the daily greeting, and your details appear on summary reports and PDFs.

Emergency Contacts

Add phone numbers for your oncology unit, out-of-hours service, and GP. These appear on the Today screen for quick access ‚Äì tap to dial.

Cycle Dates

Enter the Day 0 (chemotherapy day) for each cycle as you get scheduled. The app calculates all other days automatically. You can set up to 6 cycles.

Medications

View all your scheduled medications. You can:

  • Add new medications with the + button
  • Edit any medication by tapping it or the Edit link
  • Delete medications with the üóëÔ∏è Delete link
  • Reset to the default schedule if needed

Each medication shows which times of day it's taken (checkboxes) and the day range.

Appearance

Toggle between dark and light mode, show or hide daily messages, and choose an accent colour that appears throughout the app.

Daily Summary

Tap "Send Daily Summary Email" to generate a report of the day's data. This opens your email app with a pre-filled message containing:

  • Patient name, date of birth, and patient/hospital number
  • Date and cycle day information
  • All medications with taken/not taken status and times
  • PRN medications used
  • Symptom scores
  • Temperature and weight
  • Notes, food & drink, and today's win
Tip
Send the daily summary to a family member or caregiver to keep them informed, or use it as a record for clinic appointments.

Backup & Restore

Why Back Up?

Your data is stored locally in your browser. It can be lost if you clear your browser data, switch devices, or reset your phone. Regular backups protect against this.

Export Backup

Downloads a JSON file containing all your data ‚Äì medications, tracking history, settings, everything. Save this file somewhere safe like iCloud, Google Drive, or email it to yourself.

Import Backup

Restore from a previously exported backup file. This replaces your current data.

Clear All Data

Removes all tracking data permanently. Use with caution ‚Äì this cannot be undone.

Recommendation
Back up at least once a week, or at the end of each cycle. The daily summary emails also serve as a partial backup of each day's data.

Tips for Using Day by Day

Build a Routine
Check the app at the same times each day ‚Äì perhaps when you take your morning medications, after lunch, and before bed.
Log in Real Time
Mark medications as taken when you actually take them, not later. This gives you accurate timestamps and helps avoid confusion about what you've taken.
Use the Notes
Jot down anything that might be relevant ‚Äì unusual symptoms, questions for your doctor, what you ate, how you slept. These notes can be invaluable at appointments.
Review Before Appointments
Before seeing your oncologist, look back through your tracked data, daily summaries, and cycle trend charts. This helps you give accurate information about how the cycle has gone.
Celebrate Small Wins
The "Today's Win" field isn't just for show. Noting positive moments, however small, can genuinely help maintain perspective during a challenging time.
